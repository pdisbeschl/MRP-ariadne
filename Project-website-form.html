Making Rigorous Numerics Easy

Student(s): Paul Disbeschl, Eugenia Gorbulenko, Anna Koufou, Camilla Lummerzheim, Daniel Wolters; Supervisor(s): Dr. Pieter Collins; Semester: 2019-2020;

Problem statement and motivation:

In all branches of engineering, verification is a very important step to make sure that the designed product works as expected in every situation. Instead of testing everything on the real system, it is also possible to perform the tests on a computer instead. Therefore, a mathematical model of the system has to be formulated that captures every influencing factor. This is a very specific use case where the calculations have to be of high accuracy so it cannot be implemented easily by standard libraries. Ariadne is a library specifically designed for the cases described above. To ensure accuracy, the calculations are performed rigorously. It is written in C++ but also has bindings to be used in Python. The rigorous numerics methods can be used outside of a model checking context as well. At the moment, it is a challenge to get started with Ariadne because of the documentation. Parts of it are out of date, difficult to comprehent and useful information for beginners is often missing. By improving it and adding missing parts, it could be more accessible to students and other people who might benefit from it.
